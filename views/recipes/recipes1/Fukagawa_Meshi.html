About the Dish

Origin: Fukagawa Meshi, a dish from the Edo period, was a quick meal for fishermen in Tokyo's Fukagawa area.
Flavor: Rice cooked with clams and miso broth, delivering a rich umami taste with a touch of the sea.
Cultural Significance: A symbol of Edo’s culinary heritage, highlighting the abundance of Tokyo Bay clams.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 200g clams (asari), cleaned
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1/2 leek, sliced
  • 1 cup dashi broth
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp sake
  • 1/2 tsp sugar

Instructions

  1. Wash and cook clams until they open; remove and strain the broth.
  2. Remove clams from shells, keeping the meat intact.
  3. Make soup base with clam broth, miso, sake, mirin, sugar, and ginger.
  4. Add sliced carrots, green onions, and fried tofu to the broth.
  5. Simmer, return clams to the pot, and serve over steamed rice.
