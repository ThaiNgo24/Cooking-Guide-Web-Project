About the Dish

Origin: Hiyashi Chuka is a Japanese take on Chinese noodles, popular since the mid-20th century.
Flavor: Cold ramen noodles with a tangy soy dressing, topped with cucumber, egg, and ham.
Cultural Significance: A refreshing summer dish in Japan, perfect for hot weather.

Ingredients

  • 200g Chinese-style egg noodles (chuka soba)
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1 egg, cooked into a thin omelet and sliced
  • 50g ham or cooked chicken, sliced
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp mustard (optional)
  • Prepare the toppings:
    • Julienne cucumber and ham.
    • Cut tomato into wedges.
    • Blanch bean sprouts.

Instructions

  1. Cook the noodles: Boil dried Chuka Men or ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and cool them under cold running water. Once cooled, strain well and set aside.
  2. Make the sauce: Mix together soy sauce, rice vinegar, water, sugar, and sesame oil in a bowl.
  3. Assemble the dish: Place the cold noodles on a plate, arrange the toppings on top, and pour the sauce over everything.
  4. Serve: Optionally, add Japanese mustard (Karashi) for a spicy kick.
